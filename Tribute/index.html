Dr. Norman Borlaug

Renowned plant pathologist credited with the Green Revolution.

An American agricultural scientist, humanitarian, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate who is credited with saving over a billion lives by developing high-yielding varieties of wheat during the Green Revolution.

Timeline of Dr. Norman Borlaug's Life

  • 1914: Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1937: Earns his Ph.D. in Plant Pathology from the University of Minnesota
  • 1944: Begins working in Mexico to develop disease-resistant wheat varieties
  • 1960s: The Green Revolution takes effect, significantly increasing wheat yields globally
  • 1970: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize
